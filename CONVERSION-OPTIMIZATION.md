## Site Architecture Understanding

**This is a NEWS SITE. Not a book landing page.**

Model: oilprice.com with their "Free Energy Report" banners.

The book appears as:
- Sticky header/sidebar banner
- In-article CTAs (contextual)
- Footer CTA block
- Exit-intent popup (optional)

**User journey:** Come for news → see banner → convert (or not) → keep reading

This changes everything about how we optimize.

---

## Executive Summary

The current content strategy is **educational-first** — clean, credible, no hype. That's the right foundation for a news site.

**Core Problem:** Banner CTAs have ~2 seconds to convert. The current copy is informational ("150 pages on tokenized gold") when it needs to be **interruptive** and **benefit-dense**.

**The Fix:** Write banners that stop the scroll. Hook with curiosity or dream outcome. Make the click feel inevitable.

---

## The Banner Psychology

**News readers are NOT looking for your book.** They came for the article. The book banner is an interruption.

That means:
- You have **2 seconds** to hook attention
- Copy must be **scannable** (5-10 words max for headline)
- Benefit must be **immediate and obvious**
- CTA must feel **low-friction**

**The oilprice.com model works because:**
1. Reader is already interested in energy/commodities
2. "Free report" feels like insider access
3. Banner appears contextually relevant to what they're reading

We replicate this with digital gold content.

## 3. CTA Button Copy — Quick Reference

### The Rules for Banner CTAs

1. **3 words max** for button text
2. **Action verb first** — Get, Download, Send, Start
3. **Avoid generic** — "Submit," "Subscribe," "Click Here"
4. **Arrow adds momentum** — "Get It →" beats "Get It"

### Universal CTA Rewrites

| Location | Before | After |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Header button | "Get the Free Book" | "Free Book →" |
| Sidebar banner | "[Get the Book →]" | "[Download Free]" |
| In-article CTA | "[Download the Book]" | "[Get It Now →]" |
| Article footer | "[Download Free PDF]" | "[Download Free →]" |
| Newsletter form | "[Subscribe]" | "[Subscribe Free →]" |
| Exit popup primary | — | "[Send Me the Book]" |
| Exit popup secondary | — | "[Just the PDF →]" |
| Mobile sticky bar | — | "[Get It]" or "[Free ↓]" |

## 5. Lead Magnet Strategy — News Site Context

### The Book as Banner Magnet

On a news site, the book serves a different purpose than on a landing page:

**Landing page:** Book IS the product. Optimize everything for it.
**News site:** Book is the BRIBE. It converts readers into leads.

### Why "No Email Required" Is Wrong Here

The current strategy says "no email required" multiple times. This is a mistake.

**On a landing page:** No-email reduces friction, may increase downloads.
**On a news site:** You're interrupting their reading. If they click, they're interested. Capture the lead.

**The math:**
- 10,000 visitors
- 500 click "Download" (5%)
- With email gate: 300 submit (60% of clickers) = 300 leads
- Without email: 450 download (90% of clickers) = 0 leads

**300 leads > 0 leads.**

The 150 extra downloads without email are valueless if you can't contact them.

### Recommended Approach: Soft Gate

**Primary path (80% of banner real estate):**
```
[your@email.com          ] [Send Me the Book →]
```

**Secondary path (small text below):**
```
or download without email →
```

This captures most leads while giving skeptics an out.

### Content Upgrades (Article-Specific Magnets)

Beyond the main book, offer article-specific downloads:

| Article Topic | Content Upgrade |
|---------------|-----------------|
| PAXG explainer | "PAXG vs XAUT comparison sheet" |
| BIV calculation | "BIV calculator spreadsheet" |
| Gold price analysis | "2026 gold price factors checklist" |
| NatGold intro | "NatGold due diligence checklist" |
| Portfolio strategy | "Digital gold allocation worksheet" |

**Format:**
```
───────────────────────────────────────────────────
📎 DOWNLOAD: PAXG vs XAUT Comparison Sheet
One-page breakdown of fees, custody, and redemption.
[your@email.com          ] [Send It →]
───────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

These convert higher than generic book CTAs because they're **contextually relevant** to what the reader just consumed.

## 6. Friction Points — Banner Context

### Banner-Specific Friction

| Friction | Why It Matters | Fix |
|----------|----------------|-----|
| "150 pages" | Sounds like homework | Use "Free guide" or "Free playbook" in banners |
| Multi-field forms | Too much for a banner | Email only. Name is optional. |
| Unclear delivery | "Will it email? Download?" | "Instant download" or "Sent to your inbox" |
| No social proof in banner | No credibility signal | Add "47K+ downloads" |
| Privacy concerns | Email = spam worry | Add "No spam" directly below field |
| Book cover image missing | No visual anchor | Add thumbnail in sidebar banners |

### Banner Copy Constraints

**Sidebar banner:** ~100 characters max for body copy
**In-article CTA:** ~150 characters max
**Header button:** 3 words max
**Mobile sticky bar:** ~40 characters + button

Write for these constraints. Every word must earn its place.

### Email Form Best Practices

```
✓ Single field (email only)
✓ Placeholder text: "your@email.com"
✓ Button: action verb + benefit ("Send Me the Book")
✓ Below field: "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."
✓ Social proof nearby: "47,382 downloads"

✗ Don't ask for name (adds friction)
✗ Don't use "Submit" (generic)
✗ Don't hide privacy info (breeds suspicion)
✗ Don't require confirmation checkbox (legal overkill for free content)
```
